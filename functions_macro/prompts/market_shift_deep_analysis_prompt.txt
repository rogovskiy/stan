Canonical Driver Definition

You must extract a canonical driver that defines the structural cause of the shift.

The canonical driver is the stable identity of the MarketShift and will be used later to merge related news events.

Structure

The canonical driver should describe:

Actor/Event → Constraint or Structural Condition → Primary Market Channel

Rules

• Write one concise sentence (8–15 words)
• Focus on the structural cause, not market reactions
• Avoid downstream effects (inflation, equities, analyst revisions, central bank responses)
• Avoid mentioning price movements
• Avoid temporary context such as “this quarter” or “following strong earnings”
• The driver should remain valid even as the narrative evolves
• If multiple articles describe different manifestations of the same underlying driver, generalize slightly to capture the common cause
• Do not over-generalize into vague drivers like "economic uncertainty"

You must also output a short rationale (1–3 sentences) explaining why you selected this canonical driver: which evidence or pattern across the sources led to this formulation, and why it is the stable structural cause rather than a surface-level narrative.

Example

Good canonical driver:

"Iran escalation raises risk of disruption to oil shipments through the Strait of Hormuz."

Bad examples:

"Stocks fall due to Middle East tensions."
"Companies lower expectations after strong earnings."
"Economic uncertainty increases."

• Reuters

Avoid:

• category pages
• undated content
• general commentary unless it reports a new factual development

If you cannot find an article supporting a development, omit that development.
